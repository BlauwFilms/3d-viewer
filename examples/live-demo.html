Behind the scenes

Making a helmet with a lot of damage.

A short piece about rebuilding a weathered sci-fi helmet in glTF, embedded directly in the post so you can inspect every polygon.

By the Blauw Films team · 4 min read

One of the first things we decided when starting this project was that readers shouldn't have to leave a blog post to inspect the models we're writing about. If an article mentions a texture, a silhouette, or an animation cycle, it should be right there in the flow of the prose.

What you're looking at above is a physically-based rendered glTF scene, streamed over the network and composited on top of this very page. The viewer is a <div class="bview-embed"> tag that the site-wide Blauw loader script upgraded in place.

Same viewer, no chrome, auto-rotate on load

Sometimes you don't want a play button or a control bar — you just want the object to sit there and quietly spin like a jewel in a display case. Setting data-no-ui="true" and data-autoplay="true" does exactly that.

Both embeds share the same engine code. The first reader to press play on any viewer on the page pays the download cost; every other viewer on the page — including this silent second one — borrows from the same loaded Three.js instance.
